Smarter energy choices for Aussie homes

Appliances account for a significant portion of household electricity use in Australia. Choosing efficient models and using them wisely can shrink bills and emissions.

Read the energy rating label first

The Australian Government's Energy Rating Label helps compare appliance efficiency.

  • More stars indicate lower energy use for typical operation.
  • Check the annual kWh figure to estimate running costs.
  • Match appliance size and usage patterns rather than chasing stars alone.

Adopt everyday energy-smart habits

  • Fridges: Right-size capacity, set 3-5 °C, and clean door seals.
  • Washing: Cold water cycles save roughly 80–90% versus hot.
  • Dishwashers: Use eco modes and only run when full.
  • Standby: Smart power boards help cut standby or "vampire" loads.

Know where the energy goes

Heating, cooling, and hot water still dominate average household consumption, yet electronics and appliances add up too — particularly TVs, refrigeration, and laundry.
