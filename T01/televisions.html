Televisions: picture quality without the power bill sting

Modern TVs vary widely in energy use. Key factors include technology (OLED vs LED), screen size, brightness settings, and daily usage hours.

Quick wins

  • Enable Energy Saving / Eco mode.
  • Reduce backlight/brightness to suit the room.
  • Use auto power-down and sleep timers.
  • Disable always-listening voice assistants if unused.

Choosing an efficient TV

  • Compare the Energy Rating Label and annual kWh.
  • For the same size, newer LED models usually use less than old plasma/LCD.
  • Ultra-high HDR brightness can increase energy draw.
  • 55–65″ is a good balance of size vs energy for many lounges.
